Comparison of Scheduling Policies for CPU-bound Jobs
Run my_program in CPU-bound mode under CFS (Completely Fair Scheduler), RR (Round Robin), and FIFO (First-In First-Out).
Note: CFS is the default Linux scheduler. It resembles MLFQ (Multi-Level Feedback Queue) and Stride/Lottery Scheduling in concept, but is implemented as a mathematically structured algorithm balancing fairness and efficiency.
CPU-bound vs IO-like Workloads
Run my_program in both CPU-bound and IO-like modes.
Observe and compare differences under the three scheduling policies.
Effect of Priority
Run my_program under RR and FIFO, experimenting with different real-time priority values (1–99).
Run my_program under CFS, varying the nice value to see how relative priority influences scheduling.
Additional Experiments
Explore various combinations of scheduling policies and priorities.
Derive and summarize insights from your observations.
